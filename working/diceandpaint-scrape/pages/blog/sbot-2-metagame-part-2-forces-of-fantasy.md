In part 2 of this metagame review I'll be looking at the army lists from Forces of Fantasy. I'm going to talk about some general trends for the faction, then cover a list or two that I find interesting. I'm not trying to cover the best lists, nor am I trying to predict winners, just highlight some interesting choices.

## Kingdom of Bretonnia (15 Lists)

Brets are the second most popular faction at this event and people have overwhelmingly gone for Bretonnian Exiles.

#### Bretonnian Exiles (10 Lists)

9 of the exiles lists took a Outcast Wizard, the other took 3. Only 5 lists took the Green Knight, a surprise to me. 8 of the lists took 2 Bombards, the others skipped it. The lists average spending 856 points on characters, and at 65 Peg Knights we have 6.5 per list; most lists had 2 units, one had 3, and a few had one. One lists omits Banner of the Zealous Knight which is surprising as it's one of the biggest reasons to go Exiles. Likewise 3 lists omit the Falcon-horn of Fredemund, one of the best magic items in the game.

I can't not talk about this list. This is the first time I've seen three Outcast Wizards and well, I'm not sure how it plays. I suspect that they want to get a bunch of magic missiles, the list does have Bombards. However it doesn't take Ruby Ring. I'm also worried that when rolling for spells on Daemonology you'll have a real hard time using some of these on your on-foot wizards.

The list also has 3 units of Yeoman Guard, presumably at least some of these will be used as bunkers. Yeoman Guard are very good, but I don't think these medium sized units will be able to do much in combat nor hold out very long. The other head scratcher is that the BSB is on foot. I assume he goes into the largest Yeoman unit getting it +2 static res but I'm not impressed, the unit is still very venerable to shooting. I'd like it more if one Yeoman unit was dropped to beef up the main one.

Outside of those choices it's a pretty typical list. The single character Pegasus Knights unit will be less killy than most, and it's also 7 models, before the Baron joins, that starts to be pretty hard to maneuver. I think it's a pretty interesting take on Exiles but I'm not sure if it it will perform well.

Here I'm including what may be my favorite Exiles list. This list is pretty focused on what it wants to do, and the builder has picked the right stuff. We've taken Falcon Horn and the Banner of the Zealous Knight of course. Two characters will join the Peg Knights while the Green Knight will do Green Knight things, and an outcast wizard to join or hide behind the core infantry. What I really like is that core. A single unit of 6 Knights of the Realm has been taken, and honestly, I could lose these. Knights of the Realm are not terrible core, but they're generally the worst unit in any competitive Bret list. I find it pretty easy to pick up their not insubstantial points, at 24 points per wound anything that can lower their armor save will easily take the unit down. To make up half of their core we've got a unit of 35 Yeoman Guard, these are a pretty great infantry unit and their combination of rules and the size of this unit means it will be quite hard for your opponent to shift them or pick up these 250 points.

I think this is just a great list, the one inclusion I question is the Trebuchet, I'm not really sure what the purpose is and feel like the points could be better used elsewhere.

## Wood Elves (11 Lists)

I was not expecting this many wood elves, but as I said in the previous article the fact that the first SBOT was won by them has to play a factor. Looking across the lists some high level things of note:  
5 Dragons  
11 level 4s (one list with 2, one skipping it)  
23 Deepwood Scouts units (19 in core, 4 in special)  
17 Glade Guard units  
25 Sisters of the Thorn units  
11 Wild Rider units  
13/17 casters taking Battle Magic

Looking at this it becomes clear that most Wood Elf lists will start with a level 4, 2 units of Deepwood Scouts in core, and 2-3 units of Sisters of the Thorn. Which all makes sense, those are all very good things. I'm a little surprised there are not a couple more dragons, Wood Elves are a very squishy list but have a very survivable dragon, I feel like having one gives you a lot more wiggle room on your play, though I think the dragon-less builds are probably a bit stronger if played perfectly.

Empire representation in SBOT2 is very middle of the pack, about what I'd expect for a popular faction that has limited competitive list building options. Empire lists took a lot of level 4s, 12 of them in these 8 armies. And that's in large part thanks to 2 lists packing 3 of them. But that's not all Empire spammed, we have 17 Engineers, 17 Great Cannons, 12 Helblaster Volley Guns, and 21 Empire Archer units. Now on the other hand I'm very surprised to see only 1 Imperial Griffon, 2 units of Demigryph Knights, and 4 Steam Tanks. With only one unit of 5 Flagellants across all 8 lists I do expect empire to really struggle into dragons.

Well first off we have our three level 4 wizards, all taking battle magic, all with mounts but no units they can join or hit behind. That's very interesting and means he's relying on hiding his wizards out of LoS from the enemy, I get pretty nervous about this as all it takes is a good ranged unit on a hill, or a wizard on a dragon and your level 4s need to hide behind terrain. But they do give him a lot of firepower. Then core is a mix of Empire Archers and state Missile Troops, no melee combat here. Then we have the same 5 war machines as the last list. But the really cute thing here is the list has spent 150 points on melee models, a trio of mercenary Doomseekers. Empire melee options kind of suck so I like seeing the Doomseekers come in here. They'll likely not do anything but in dying their points are denied.

So yeah, these two lists kinda sum up how I feel about Empire, it's just like a gun line. Which is a bummer, I really hope the Arcane Journal gives them some more interesting playstyles.

## Dwarfen Mountain Holds (8 Lists)

Like Empire above I think Dwarves are a very popular faction, but one with some playstyle limitations that will keep their presence down. What they do have here is a very good spread across their army lists with 3 Grand Army and Expeditionary Force, and 2 Royal Holds. So with that I'm expecting Dwarf lists to be the most diverse ones we've looked at so far. I'm going to lump all the armies together rather than looking at each type separately because I see a lot of overlap here and we only have a few examples for each. Some of the units I'm including on the charge below I'm including because I'm surprised there were not more.

Key Units:  
26 Gyrocopters (heavily influenced by one list taking 10)  
9 Doomseekers (3 lists taking 3)  
9 Grudge Throwers  
9 units of Irondrakes  
3 Anvil of Doom  
3 Dwarf Carts

If those numbers seem low, it's because they are. These dwarf players have taken a lot of different things and it's hard to draw a through-line on what's good or what they like. There are a lot more things like Thunderers than I expected, and I don't see a lot of big points denial units. I'd be happy to be proven wrong but I just don't expect these dwarf lists to run the tables at SBOT2.

I do want to note two trends I see. First, a lot of the melee infantry units are quite small, 8-12 models. In my experience these units will often very quickly be shot off the board or charged by a big bad and struggle to get any points back. Also interesting, a number of these lists have ranged units of 16-20 models that don't have skirmisher or volley fire. I'm not sure if the game plan for these is to linehammer them, but they seem hard to make work.

## High Elves (5 Lists)

Rounding out the Forces of Fantasy are High Elves. A very popular army in 8th I'm coming off a 29 person event with 3 High Elf lists so I'm surprised to only see 5 here. At 959 character points per list I don't think any other army will touch the high elves when it comes to herohammer. That core slot comes in with 4 Star Dragons, a Sun Dragon, and 3 Griffons. There are no double-dragon lists. We also have 6 level 4s across the lists.

In core we see a mix of Elven Archers, Silver Helms, Sisters of Avelorn, and a single unit of Ellyrian Reavers and Elven Spearmen. Sisters of Avelorn really stand out across these lists. There are 7 units of them, and two of those are 300+ points of them in Core. High Elves don't have a very deep bench so it's not surprising to see Sisters indexed into heavily.

Outside of sisters special and rare are a grab bag. There are Bolt Throwers, Giant Eagles, Skycutters, Dragon Princes, Tiranoc Chariots, but nothing in great number.

Let's start with the dragon. Rather than taking a Prince we have an Archmage on a Star Dragon. This is actually pretty gross. Then we take the very cheap Seed of Rebirth and you've got a 4+/5+/5+.
