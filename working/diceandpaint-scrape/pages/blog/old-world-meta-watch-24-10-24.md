# Old World Metawatch - Triple GTs

[Old World](/blog/category/Old+World)[Metawatch](/blog/category/Metawatch)

Oct 24

Written By [Tim](/?author=631dce765850301d086426a2)

This past weekend saw 3 North American GT events so seems like a good chance to touch base on what's doing well. Not sure how often I'll do posts like this but we'll see. I'm going to somewhat arbitrarily include any events that are 5 rounds **or** 20+ people. That adds in a 3-rounder in Sweden this past weekend. I'm not including events below 2000 points.

This is not intended as a comprehensive review but checking in on these events. Everything being discussed is based on records as shown in BCP, I don't have info on Best Overall winners when soft scores are included. I'm going to include lists that got 4-1 or better to avoid cutting off on tiebreakers or having to cover too many armies. For 3 round events it's 3-0 or bust. Lists are shown in the order they placed.

When I talk about lists below I'm looking at them from a competitive lens. Obviously every list shown did well, but I'll make some comments when I see something unexpected, or if there is something I question. I'm not listing every local metagame here but I'll make a few comments and let's start by taking a look at lists played across the four events I looked at.

![](images/blog/old-world-meta-watch-24-10-24/f043b181bcc5.webp)

You can see from the chart that a few armies are very popular with lots of representation at big events. The second largest event this weekend only had one Bretonnian player so that pulled their numbers down a bit. Unsurprisingly the legacy armies can't keep up with the fully supported armies with Vampire Counts beating Beastmen but all the other legacy armies being at the bottom of the pile. Until we get to Wood Elves who stand out for their low play rate, I'm not sure how much of that is model access, army playstyle, or just lack of popularity. Looking at alternative army lists you can see that Wolves of the Sea, Mortuary Cults, Bretonnian Exiles, and Royal Clans made up decent chunks of their factions. Some lists on the other hand were very unpopular and are crowded out of their faction.

## **Square Based Open Toronto - Toronto, ON, CA - 50 Players**

The largest event this weekend was the SBOT (hmm) taking place in Toronto. 50 players were in the event. Event page is [here](https://bestcoastpairings.com/event/52NAC48ML0), packet is [here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1-laXkufgVDXucIXr1S2tr-JoBAXYiGLT6LVzoJkGJwk/edit?usp=sharing). This event had a good spread of armies with everything but Wood Elves and Skaven showing up. However it was very heavily stacked in favor of four armies with Warriors, High Elves, Brets, and Tomb Kings making up 62% of the field. Here is a [podcast episode from Square Based](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ndse8YvM7MU) on the event. Here is a [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-GeTEM_gZU0) from 2pluspodcast looking at the event as well.

#### The Packet

The tournament packet shouldn't have many surprises for those who played in the SBOT online tournament. Rounds are 2:45 which I like and think will become more common. Comp was rule of 6 (core)/3, and no allies. Monsters give up 25% of their points at 25% of their wounds remaining which is a pretty conservative take on scoring monster points but matches units from the core rulebook. Green Knight likely gives up 25% if he's come back from the dead. There is one thing that stands out which is 50 VP for killing or running a T6 monster off the board. In a packet light on comp or rules changes this frankly very small monster nerf is strange. Missions are more or less out of the core book.

Overall I don't love the packet, doesn't address any of 8th's issues and the one very light bit of comp has the typical "doesn't hurt Bretonnia but actually helps them." They didn't do great overall but I suspect that was a lack of experienced players with the army when compared to events in the UK or the Mid Atlantic of the US.

One last comment on this event, I love having players put the number of deployment drops on their list, makes it a lot easier to keep track of that during deployment hopefully saying a minute or two of players tallying their drops.

#### List Commentary

Warriors took the top two spots the first place list is a pretty typical Dragon & Friends army though Adam has not taken any of the new rare units from the Arcane Journal. The second place list is Wolves of the Sea and there are a couple interesting things. Chris took a Daemon Prince rather than a Chaos Lord so he does have a level 4 and it's pretty focused on casting. Then he's taken a 428 point Exalted Champion on a Gorebeast Charriot. This is pretty neat as something like a mini-dragon. My big concern here is the lack of Fly and the lack of Swiftstride, two of the better dragon rules. But this is still an interesting piece of tech for Wolves of the Sea lists with their limited character access.

For the 4-1 lists the Beastmen list followed a theme of the weekend in not seeing Gigantic Chaos Spawn in Beastmen list, I suspect this will change over the coming months. It did load up on Dragon Ogres which I think is probably one of the things Beastmen want to load up on. The Exiles list is a bit unusual, it looks very normal until you see there are only 777 points of characters. The saved points allow Caleb to take take two Bombards and two units of 5 Pegasus Knights, looks like this mostly worked out well for him as I'm sure having access to the Green Knight helped.

The Mortuary Cult list is pretty unusual. Charlotte elected to take double Elementalism level 4s and then 6 Ushabti Great Bows in core AND in special. This is a lot of shooting. I'm a little confused by the Scarab Broach and I wonder if it was a mistake. The High Priest with the Broach can't join the Ushtabi and there are no Skeleton Archers in the list and it doesn't boost the character's magic missiles. As a huge fan of Skeleton Warriors I wonder if that unit should have been archers. More broadly, this list is looking to shoot the enemy to bits, Lore Familiar on the L2 Illusion BSB has to be for charge control and I like it as a bit of tech. Double Caskets must be there mostly for Light of Protection and honestly, that's totally worth it. It's an interesting take on the list and I'm glad it did well for her.

The first High Elf list is another list that isn't similar to what I'd expect. Andy didn't take a dragon and instead has taken 4 characters including an Archmage on a Lion Chariot and another Archmage on foot. I've not played a double level 4 High Elf list and would be interested to see it in action. Outside of characters it's more what I'd expect with a lot of mounted units in core, a lot of Sisters of Avelorn, and some Swordmasters that will likely be joined by a wizard.

Rounding out the 4-1 lists we have a very typical Dragon lord Chaos list, and a more typical High Elf list with a dragon. The rest of the High Elf list has a few interesting things including a Skycutter for the Archmage which I like, and then a 511 point block of Sea Guard in core. The Sea Guard will be a mix of points denial being fairly hard to shift, and very strong versus dragons who will take a Stand and Shoot from 33 bows if they charge the unit, the combat res bonus for that will mean the Sea Guard win combat.

## Battle At the Beltway - Arlington, VA, USA - 29 players

Taking place just outside Washington DC I attended this event and will have a little more info on it. I'll probably write more about this later. The event page is [here](https://www.bestcoastpairings.com/event/TEUR51UD38), and the packet is [here](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1SVxMbpKIPZA98GA-1WEVz388b7M6lbbc/view?usp=sharing). The meta for this one was heavy on Warriors with 6 players, two taking Wolves of the Sea. Tomb Kings were in second with 4 players. Then a bunch of armies had 1-3 players.

#### The Packet

I really liked the packet for this one. There was a comp/theme score for best overall but no list building restrictions. However there was a big change in the introduction of Scoring Units. This was any unit that is not in skirmish, not a monster, and started the game at US 9 or higher. A lot of missions rewarded these units and I think it worked well. Missions were from the rulebook but with some tweaks and then bonus objectives up to 5 points making each round have a possible 25 points. I especially want to call out For Honor & Glory which let each player select one bonus ability, this was for round 5 and I think everyone had a really good time having this little tweak to their list for that game.

#### List Commentary

In first place we have Phil and his Tomb King Grand Army. Phil plays in a lot of events and does very well. It's a pretty typical double-dragon list for the Tomb Kings forgoing Casket of Souls for two Necrosphinxes giving it 4 big flying threats. Luna another east coast powerhouse, took second with her Exiles again a pretty typical list, 2 bombards, a green knight, a pegasus knight unit with 2 characters, and a level 3 wizard. Chuck took third with Vampire Counts and I think had the most interesting list here. He didn't take Mortis Engines or screams and loaded up on a big Blood Knight bus while also taking some rarely seen units like Black Knights. I was talking to another Vampire Count player and we agreed that VC are deeper than just scream lists so it's nice to see this.

## **SoCal Open 2024 -** Del Mar, CA, USA - 14 players

On the small side for a GT but it's great to see some west coast events. Event listing is [here](https://bestcoastpairings.com/event/WYG33EHMF3), player packet is [here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1-p3G6RBsJ5wfA5IYy8bGIYlZ90U0MlrGwFQQPBICLcc/edit?usp=sharing). No army was repeated more than twice and most were represented so it was a nice spread for a small event.

#### The Packet

Pretty typical packet, rule of 3/6, no allies, no comp. Missions are from the core book but they did include rules for Arcane Ruins. They function like forests but give MR 2 to units inside them. I actually like this a lot, having more options to fight magic in Old World is a good thing. Not changing any scoring I'm less a fan of and I feel like this type of basic packet heavily favors the top armies in the meta.

#### List Commentary

The High Elf list in first place had two Moon Dragons. Core was mostly a big block of Sea Guard. Then interestingly his rare was a pair of Lion Chariots and Skycutters. I've not played against the Lion Chariots but overall I like this list a lot. It has a lot of speed and the big block of Sea Guard as the only slow unit in your army will try and function as an anti-dragon deterrence and points denial.

The Warriors list has a dragon lord, some typical core though there is a large unit of Chaos Warriors. In rare there is one Gigantic Spawn and two Gorebeast Charriots. I'm not sure if it was a model availability thing but I'd rather have seen two Spawn.

The Exiles list here again skimps on characters and uses the points to take 2 units of Pegasus Knights. However one is only three models which I think is pretty hard to use effectively as the unit only has 6 wounds. Other than that it's what you'd expect, 2 Bombards, plenty of Knights of the Realm.

## **The Return - Partille, Sweden - 20 Players**

Sweden has always been big in fantasy so I think it's worth keeping an eye on their events. The event page is [here](https://bestcoastpairings.com/event/UHLNKK5L5P?), and the packet (in Swedish) is [here](https://drive.google.com/file/d/10TKnrx5Cs_LTuHiaq9K75jAKDSlrJbUO/view?usp=sharing). The event had a really nice mix of armies with only 3 factions having more than one player. Orcs and Goblins was in first with 3 players, the Swedes are doing Old World right.

#### The Packet

No comp or list building changes, missions were also directly out of the rulebook. Looks like this was held in a school cafeteria which is neat.

#### List Commentary

The Orc & Goblin list is a bit of everything. It takes 997 points of characters but skips a wyvern. The general is a Black Orc Warboss in a chariot which will be a hard hitting unit and is pretty tanky while coming in about 1000 points under a wyvern character. The BSB is a Bigboss who will go in a small Black Orc unit. Then three Night Goblin Warbosses will join a unit of Night Goblins with 3 Fanatics. I'm not sure how that unit will do in combat but it will probably be able to pull out some surprising combat results for Night Goblins. Special and rare is a mix of war machines and monsters.

The Mortuary Cults list is mostly a leadership bomb list. It has 2 level 4s with necromancy/illusion. I think it's a little weaker as it then splits up things. Core has a small unit of bow Ushabti, a small unit of Skeleton Archers, a big unit of Skeleton Warriors. Special has a Necrosphinx and a unit of Carrion which I've not played yet but seem like they'd be good in some matchups. Then in rare we only have one Casket and one Catapult. Overall I think this is a list that lacks focus and would be improved by leaning into some units harder, but it obviously worked out for Anders.
