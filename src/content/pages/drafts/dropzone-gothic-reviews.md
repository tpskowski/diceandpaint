Mostly because I thought it'd be interesting to look at two products with a lot of overlap. Battletech is a 6mm war game about giant robots, Battletech: Gothic

# Packaging and Unboxing

## Operation Artemis
The box this comes in is perfectly servicable, however it is not the glossy, high quality box of Battletech: Gothic. This is a box you're happier to put in the recycling, though if you wanted to use it for storage it will probably hold up a while. When you open it the first thing you notice is the sprues are in plastic bags, presumably to protect them, though I don't think this is needed. Amusingly, the bases which are not in bags had one fall off, however this really isn't a problem at all.

The biggest issue I had with the initial expereince here is there are no assembly instructions in the box, I'm fine with that, I can look up a PDF. But there is also no sheet saying "go here for assembly instructions". This isn't crucial but it helps make things clear what your next step is. If I were in charge of this product I'd put all four sprues in one ziplock bag, that should be plenty of attention, and then just a small black and white sheet of paper saying assembly instructions are online and add a URL and QR code.

### Assembly
The organic fauna models are kind of rough in this regard. TTCombat plastics are primarily ships and vehicles so I was worried on this point and those concerns unfortunately paid off. 

The instructions start with the mid-sized models, the Sphinxes. They're only six parts which is good, the issue is the legs are 2 parts and don't have great engineering. The place where the shin connects to the knee is pretty freeform for 2/4 legs. This is compounded by the fact that the legs have a variety of poses they can go in. When assembling these I recommend gluing the legs together and let them fully try before attaching to the torso. On top of all of that there are some gaps between leg and torso, at least as mine are assembled.

The Minotaur, the largest model in the kit is nine models which again is a pretty good part count. Assembly on this one is pretty smooth but you will have some notable gaps, they're easy to to fill with sprue goo so that's what I'd recommend.

The instructions end there, you've got the 5 flyers per sprue, but those are one-part models which just go on a flying stand.
